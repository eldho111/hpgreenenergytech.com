Why we exist

From diesel-dependence to a liquid-clean India

We started with a simple field observation: the places that most need reliable energy— fishing harbors, inland waterways, village microgrids—often burn the dirtiest fuel. Our founders prototyped compact stacks in a small lab, then hauled them to where boats dock and autos queue. What we learned shaped our design: fast refuel, rugged hardware, and financeable rollouts beat lab records every time.

Today, we’re building modular electrolyzers that fit India’s reality: turbulent renewables, tight budgets, and maintenance in the wild. The goal isn’t a demo—it’s displacement of diesel at scale.

Mission

Deliver bankable H₂/NH₃ energy assets that replace diesel in mobility, marine, and microgrids—without asking operators to change how they work.

Vision

A liquid-clean economy where green hydrogen and ammonia flow like today’s fuel, stabilizing the grid and powering livelihoods from coasts to hills.

How we’ll get there
  • Field-first engineering: Design for docks, depots, and dusty roads.
  • Ammonia-ready stacks: Liquid logistics with minimal behavior change.
  • RE-native systems: Curtailment-aware control and seasonal storage.
  • Bankability: Standardized skids, warranties, and service playbooks.

Products

PiperION® AEM Membrane (40 μm)

Self-supporting anion exchange membrane for water electrolysis, CO₂ reduction, and AEM fuel cells. High ionic conductivity and alkaline durability.

Specs
  • Ionic conductivity ≥ 60 mS/cm
  • Continuous operation up to ~80 °C
  • Durability testing > 1000 h

AEL / AEM Anode Electrodes

Ni–Fe / Co-based OER electrodes on SS/Ti supports for alkaline and AEM electrolysis; low polarization resistance and long life.

Specs
  • High current efficiency (>95%)
  • Custom geometries for lab → pilot → MW
  • Reduced noble-metal dependency
